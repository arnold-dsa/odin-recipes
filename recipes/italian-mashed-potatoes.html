Italian Mashed Potatoes

The Best Meatloaf Recipe

What makes these Italian mashed potatoes Italian, you ask? It's the combination of olive oil in the boiling water, Parmigiano and scamorzza cheese, and a quick bake to melt all of the goodness in.

We're not going to argue whether or not the Potato is Italians's favorite vegetable; but what we can say is that it is certainly one of our favorite budget-friendly, easy to transform vegetables and the star of today's Easy Potato Recipes roundup! Ingridients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 small golden potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley, or to taste
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmigiano, plus more for the top
  • 1 cup shredded scarmozza cheese
  • 1⅓; cup plain breadcrumbs, or to taste
  • 12 tablespoons butter, cut into pieces, or to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C).
  2. Bring a pot of salted water with the olive oil to a boil. Then add the potatoes; cook until tender, about 15 minutes. Reserve 1 cup water, and drain potatoes. Transfer to a mixing bowl.
  3. Mash the potatoes, adding some reserved water to soften and fluff potatoes. You will not need the whole cup of water.
  4. BFold in parsley, Parmigiano, and scarmozza. Season with salt.
  5. Transfer to an 8x8-inch baking dish and spread evenly. Sprinkle with breadcrumbs, grate a little Parmigiano over breadcrumbs, and scatter butter pieces on top.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until the top is golden, about 20 minutes.
